Park Rules

A few simple rules keep Country Horizon a quiet, well-kept place for everyone.

  1. Quiet Hours

    Quiet hours run from 10:00 PM to 7:00 AM. Please keep noise to a minimum out of respect for your neighbors.

  2. Speed Limit

    The speed limit throughout the park is 5–10 mph. Please don't peel out or spin gravel.

  3. Responsible Guests

    The person registered for a site is financially responsible for the conduct of their guests.

  4. Pets

    Pets must be leashed at all times when outside your RV and never left unattended. Please clean up after your pet — owners are responsible for any damage or injury their pet causes.

  5. Children

    Children must be supervised by a parent or guardian at all times. Parents and guardians are responsible for any damage caused by their children.

  6. Trash

    Please place all trash in the provided dumpster. Don't leave trash outside your RV, and keep your site neat and clean.

  7. Cigarettes

    Please extinguish cigarettes fully and dispose of them properly — never on the ground.

  8. Water Leaks

    Please report any water leaks right away. Excessive water waste is not permitted and must be fixed promptly.

  9. Drugs

    Illegal substances are strictly prohibited on the property and will result in immediate removal from the park.

Location & Directions

Country Horizon RV Park sits in the quiet countryside of Ripley, Oklahoma, right at the intersection of HWY 33 and HWY 108 — easy highway access for RVs and heavy trucks alike. We're about 7 miles (10–15 minutes) from Cushing, OK, a major oil pipeline hub, making us a convenient home base for pipeline and oil-field crews working monthly contracts. We're also a short drive from Stillwater (home of Oklahoma State University) and neighboring Perkins, putting travelers, OSU visitors, and RVers passing through central Oklahoma within easy reach.

Do you have 50 amp hookups?

Yes. Every one of our 9 graveled sites is 35 feet wide with full hookups, including both 30-amp and 50-amp electric service, well water, and sewer.

How far is the park from Cushing, OK?
